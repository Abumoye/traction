From The Case Files

A look at how advisory engagements actually run, drawn from real work.

Hospitality Venue — Nightclub Joint Venture

Negotiation

A lounge inside an established hotel, being reimagined as a nightclub under a new operator. We prepared the full negotiation position, opening terms through to walk-away terms, across eight deal points, and identified bar revenue and beverage control as the highest-margin point worth holding firm on. The engagement closed with a signed Memorandum of Understanding under Nigerian law, with arbitration seated in Abuja.

Family-Owned Holding Company — Long-Term Vesting Structure

Structuring

A vesting agreement built to hold for twenty years needs to be right the first time. We structured a dual-tranche vesting arrangement, split across years one to five and years six to ten, with claims only exercisable at the five and ten-year milestones, and a twenty-year sale restriction from incorporation to protect the structure long after the advisory engagement ends.

Restaurant Business — Dormant Contract Review

Contract Strategy

Not every advisory question is about growth. Sometimes it is about deciding whether a stalled agreement is worth reviving. We reviewed the dormancy, recommended an activation-then-terminate strategy, and drafted the formal activation letter with a fourteen-business-day response deadline tied to the relevant contract clause.

Common Questions

How do I know when my business needs a business advisor?

Common signals include scaling faster than your current systems can handle, facing an operational problem you cannot see the root cause of, weighing a new market or product decision, or sitting on a deal or negotiation that is too consequential to get wrong. If a decision is big enough that getting it wrong would cost real time or money, that is usually the point to bring in outside advisory support.

Should I choose a large advisory firm or a smaller one?

Large firms offer breadth across many specialties. Smaller, hands-on firms like Traction Outsourcing tend to offer closer, more personalized attention, direct access to the people actually doing the work, and advice grounded in your specific business rather than a standardized framework. Which is right depends on whether you need broad specialist coverage or a close working relationship with your advisor.

What is business advisory?

Business advisory is independent, outside support for the decisions a business has to make about its strategy, structure, operations, and deals, brought in because those decisions are too consequential to make without a second, experienced perspective.

What is the difference between business advisory and business consulting?

In practice the terms overlap heavily. Our business advisory work covers strategic planning, organizational and operational advisory, feasibility studies, and negotiation and deal support, advice grounded in the specific business in front of us, not a generic framework applied the same way to every client.

Does Traction Outsourcing help with negotiations and deal structuring?

Yes. This includes preparing negotiation positions, structuring joint ventures and vesting arrangements, and reviewing or reactivating contracts, grounded in the specific deal rather than a generic template.

Can Traction Outsourcing help with feasibility and market studies?

Yes. Feasibility and market studies are part of the business advisory service, used to test whether a new venture, product line, or market entry idea holds up before a business commits real money to it.

Is business advisory only for large companies?

No. Growing SMEs and family-owned businesses in Nigeria are a core part of who this service is built for, not only large corporates. The scale of the engagement adjusts to the scale of the business.
